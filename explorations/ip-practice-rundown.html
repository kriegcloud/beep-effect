IP practice decisions: time tracking and docketing.

Two related questions for a solo IP practice: where should Beep help, and where should it deliberately stay out of the official record? The short answer is the same in both areas: Beep should observe, explain, and propose; the lawyer approves; established systems keep the authoritative records.

Plain-English doctrine

Beep is safest as a local-first assistant and second set of eyes. It should not quietly become the billing ledger, trust ledger, or docket of record.

Time Tracking

Capture the work. Do not own the ledger.

Best first target: a solo IP attorney who wants better time capture, cleaner narratives, and flat-fee profitability insight without moving billing or trust accounting into Beep.

Verdict

Beep should be a prebill overlay.

It can collect evidence, draft entries, and help review a day of work, but approved entries should export to Clio, QuickBooks/LeanLaw, or another billing system.

Safe first move

Start with approval-gated candidates.

Agents suggest time entries and narratives. Nothing becomes billable until the attorney approves it.

Hard no for v1

No trust accounting or final invoices.

IOLTA, payments, ledgers, LEDES submission, and invoice authority stay in mature systems of record.

Docketing

Watch the deadlines. Do not become the docket.

Best first target: a US patent-prosecution-heavy solo practice where Beep can double-check official sources, explain candidate deadlines, and prove the watcher is still running.

Verdict

Beep should be a vigilance overlay.

The docket of record stays with the attorney or a specialist vendor. Beep watches sources, proposes candidates, and makes misses harder.

Safe first move

Start with narrow US rules.

Maintenance fees, office-action response periods, PCT national phase, and trademark post-registration windows are the defensible first checker set.

Hard no for v1

No broad foreign annuity engine.

Foreign renewals, discretionary dates, court-rule chains, and jurisdiction-specific grace periods belong to specialists or attorney review.
