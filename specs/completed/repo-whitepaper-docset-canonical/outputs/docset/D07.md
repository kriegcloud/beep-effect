## Reasoning Model Boundaries

1. Deterministic and schema-constrained extraction outputs are treated as primary inputs.
2. Inference layers may enrich but must not overwrite deterministic facts.
3. All inference statements are status-labeled and certainty-tier aware.
4. Conflicts between inferred and deterministic claims are escalated to governance artifacts.

## Representation Policy

### Certainty-aware representation

Represented knowledge must preserve both certainty tier and status class:

- Certainty tier: deterministic, type-system, llm-inferred.
- Status class: implemented, specified, conceptual.

### Mixed-source policy

When a claim combines deterministic and conceptual sources, the effective representation must use the more conservative status and certainty interpretation.

### Provenance policy

Inference outputs are not normative claims until linked to evidence IDs; otherwise they remain assumptions.

## Guardrails and Failure Controls

### Guardrail set

1. Budget guardrail: enforce finite token allocation for extraction/reasoning stages.
2. Latency guardrail: use stage-level timeout controls.
3. Throughput guardrail: enforce bounded concurrency.
4. Confidence guardrail: preserve certainty tiers in output labeling.
5. Governance guardrail: keep unresolved quality/audit risks visible.

### Failure controls

| Failure condition | Signal | Control action |
|---|---|---|
| Over-budget reasoning | Token allocation exceeded | Defer low-priority inference and preserve deterministic outputs. |
| Time-bounded stage failure | Stage timeout reached | Return partial result with status labels intact; do not fabricate completion. |
| Concurrency pressure | Parallel request limit reached | Queue or throttle while preserving deterministic claim integrity. |
| Governance contradiction | Inferred claim conflicts with known audit/risk findings | Route to governance documents and mark as unresolved. |

## Retrieval Tradeoffs

1. Broad retrieval can increase recall but may increase uncertainty burden and post-filtering cost.
2. Narrow retrieval improves determinism but can omit conceptual research context.
3. The strategy favors bounded retrieval with explicit status-labeled fallback for conceptual reasoning.
