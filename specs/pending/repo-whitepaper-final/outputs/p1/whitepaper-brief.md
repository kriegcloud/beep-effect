# P1 Whitepaper Brief

## Working Title

`Final White Paper: Phase-Governed Architecture, Evidence-Linked Delivery, and Risk-Explicit Operations`

## Audience

1. Executive leadership making readiness and investment decisions.
2. Technical leadership evaluating architecture, controls, and reliability posture.
3. Compliance/editorial stakeholders validating traceability and disclosure fidelity.

## Publication Objective

Deliver a publication-ready monograph that can be read as a decision document and audited as a traceability artifact.

## Reader Outcomes

After reading, target readers can:

1. Explain the program thesis and what differentiates its control architecture.
2. Evaluate system boundaries, contracts, and maturity status labels.
3. Interpret metrics and governance gates with explicit certainty boundaries.
4. Make decisions with clear visibility into unresolved risk and caveat carry.

## Narrative Arc

1. Establish thesis and context using phase-governed framing.
2. Define conceptual vocabulary and model boundaries.
3. Explain architecture, method flow, and contract surfaces.
4. Present operations and quantitative quality posture.
5. Present risk-governed roadmap and traceability annex linkage.

## Canonical Sections (S01-S10)

1. Executive Summary
2. Problem and Context
3. Conceptual Model and Terminology
4. Architecture and Dataflow
5. Methods and Reasoning
6. Interfaces and Contracts
7. Operations and Reliability
8. Validation and Metrics
9. Risks and Roadmap
10. Traceability Annex Reference

## Hard Constraints

1. Normative claims require D01-D12 claim IDs and D12 evidence IDs.
2. Status labels (`implemented`, `specified`, `conceptual`) must be preserved.
3. Certainty-tier boundaries must not be collapsed.
4. Caveats (`C-002` / `E-S03-005`, open D11 governance risk) must remain explicit.
5. Unsupported statements are assumptions, not normative claims.

## Length Target

- Main body: 7,000 to 10,000 words.
- Assumptions and annex references are excluded from main-body count.
