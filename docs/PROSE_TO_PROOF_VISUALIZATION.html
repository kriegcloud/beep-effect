response-to-office-action.md · matter: Aanstoot · grinder burr sensor · illustrative

Remarks

Claim 1 recites a burr alignment sensor that registers alignment prior to grinding. Reference 1 (to Halvorsen) instead teaches a sensor that measures grind size downstream of the grinding operation — a different function at a different stage of the apparatus.

Support for the recited limitation appears throughout the specification and is depicted in Figure 3, which shows the sensor positioned at the burr interface, registering relative burr position before any grinding occurs.

The rejection, restated

The examiner rejected claims 1–8 under §103 as obvious over Halvorsen in view of a secondary reference. The combination is said to disclose every limitation of claim 1.

Argument

Applicant respectfully traverses. The alignment sensor of claim 1 operates before grinding to set burr geometry; Halvorsen's sensor operates after, to characterize output. The references teach away from combination because Halvorsen's downstream placement presupposes a fixed burr geometry that the claimed sensor exists to establish. See Figure 3 and specification ¶0021.

Conclusion

Claims 1–8 are believed to be in condition for allowance. Reconsideration and withdrawal of the rejection are respectfully requested.

It stays on your machine

Local-first by default. Client files don't leave the device to be read; embeddings are computed on-device. A backup backend (Box · S3 · local) is a choice you make, never a default — and it holds copies, never identity.
